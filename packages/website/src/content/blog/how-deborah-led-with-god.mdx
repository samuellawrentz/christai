## How Deborah Led With God: Courage, Wisdom, and Faithful Leadership

Deborah stands as one of the most remarkable figures in the Hebrew Bible—a prophetess, judge, and military leader who heard from God and acted with unwavering courage. Her story teaches us that faithful leadership requires both listening to God and stepping forward when others hesitate.

Under the palm tree between Ramah and Bethel, Deborah held court. People traveled from across Israel to seek her wisdom and judgment. She did not inherit this role or seize it through force. God called her, and the people recognized His hand upon her life.

When Israel faced oppression under the Canaanite king Jabin and his commander Sisera, Deborah did not wait for someone else to act. She summoned Barak and delivered God's command: gather ten thousand warriors and march to Mount Tabor.

Barak's response revealed both respect and hesitation: "If you go with me, I will go. But if you don't go with me, I won't go."

Deborah agreed without hesitation. She did not rebuke Barak for his request. She understood that leadership sometimes means walking alongside those who need encouragement. She also spoke truth—the glory of victory would belong to a woman, not to Barak.

The battle unfolded exactly as God promised. Sisera's nine hundred iron chariots became useless as the Kishon River flooded. Israel prevailed. Sisera fled on foot and met his end at the hands of Jael.

Deborah's song of victory in Judges 5 reveals her heart. She praised God for the willingness of the people, honored those who answered the call, and lamented those tribes who stayed behind. She saw clearly—both the faithfulness and the failures around her.

What made Deborah effective was not position or title. It was her connection to God and her willingness to act on what He revealed. She listened. She spoke. She moved.

Many today face situations that demand courage they do not feel. Deborah's life reminds us that God often calls ordinary people to extraordinary moments. The question is not whether we feel ready. The question is whether we will trust the One who calls.
